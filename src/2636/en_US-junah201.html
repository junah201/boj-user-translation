Cheese

Problem

A rectangular board composed of square cells is covered with a thin layer of cheese (indicated by gray cells in the image below). The board’s edges have no cheese, and there may be holes within the cheese.

When the cheese is exposed to air, any cell in contact with air melts after one hour. While there’s no air inside cheese holes initially, air will enter once the surrounding cheese melts. In the example below, only the ‘c’-marked cheese cells melt after one hour, as shown in the progression from Figure 1 to Figure 2.

Figure 1 - Initial cheese layout

After another hour, only the remaining ‘c’-marked cells in Figure 2 melt, resulting in Figure 3.

Figure 2 - After one hour

Figure 3 - After two hours

In this example, it takes three hours for all the cheese to melt. During the melting process, the cheese may break into separate pieces.

Write a program to calculate the total time required for all cheese to melt and, one hour before it fully melts, the number of cheese cells that remain.

Input

The first line contains two positive integers representing the height and width of the board, with each up to 100. From the second line onward, each row contains the board's structure from top to bottom. Cells with no cheese are represented by 0, and cells with cheese are represented by 1, with a space between each number.

Output

The first line should display the total time taken for all the cheese to melt, and the second line should show the count of cheese cells remaining one hour before it fully melts.
